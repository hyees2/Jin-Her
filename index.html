  • One to One Catalogue (2023)

    I collected six large and heavy books from the collection of the best Dutch book design by Huis van het Boek. My catalog takes the form of a fold-out poster, starting at A5 and gradually expanding to A0. As the poster unfolds, it goes from a view of a specific part of the book to gradually displaying the entire book and its information. Also, when fully unfolded to A0, you can see the actual size of the book.

    The Green Lie (2022)

    The False Green Movement: Can you truly believe the eco-logos and natural landscapes on package designs? The contradictions of the companies behind these fancy eco-designs were the starting point. I create an exaggerated green image campaign, highlighting the contradictions of their fake environmentalism.

    Were it as if (2023)


    WEBSITE

    I reinterpreted the exhibition/publishing project 'WERE IT AS IF' byBik Van der Pol into a limited 2MB website. Emphasizing hidden or overlooked elements, I digitally transformed archival materials to capture the history of cultural institutions. Adjusting the grid of the exhibition presented in a multidimensional manner, I aim to enable viewers to discover the hidden layers of both the institution's history and the exhibition.

Objects in the Mirror (2023)

This book is an experimental research report that underscores how our identity is shaped through interactions with external factors and highlights the susceptibility of the images we absorb to distortion. The project was born out of curiosity for experimentation, employing a blend of digital and physical methods.

Beyond the Wheel (2023)


Full Video

The advent of artificial intelligence/robot technologies is significantly reshaping our society and environment. With the rapid advancement of AI technology, simple, repetitive tasks are gradually requiring less human intervention. However, if we completely eliminate 'simple, repetitive tasks,' what problems could arise? Moreover, what issues might arise if we remove all the (in)visible intermediate processes? Reflecting on our perception of labor in modern society, I used the example of a pottery-making process to explore how technology recognizes and transforms human tasks.

Woman sitting at the machine,thinking / Wage for house work (2023)

With chief themes of women's domestic work, social reproduction, wages, and discrimination, each of the three poems/booklets/archiving weaves together women's voices. They are based on Karen Brodine's expression of women's voices in life, Nicole Cox and Silvia Federici's strategies in the kitchen, and posters and advertisements used in the Wages for Housework campaign movements, respectively.

VisualVortex - Redefining Retail Visuals (2022)


Full Video

The grocery store leaflet, stripped of its images and text, reveals only its layout and shapes. The restructured leaflets transcend standardized forms, evolving into a more nutritious visual experience. Besides, the leaflets assembled in a 3D environment show how distracted we are by visual jungle.

444444

I drew inspiration from Exquisite Corpse to create a poetry collection generated from books. I established constraints by selecting the 4th word from the 4th line on the 4th page, counting every 4th book, and collecting from a total of 44 books. I also documented the process through a video.
